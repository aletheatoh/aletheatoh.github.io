I Am

Hi, I'm Alethea.

I am currently studying Computer Science & Sociology at Duke University. My passion lies in building software that alleviates the most unnoticed pain points, is easy to use and is aestheically pleasing. Feel free to check out my projects or my GitHub.

a fish out of water

What's with my logo? Fun fact: I perpetually feel this way - both literally and metaphorically. It began as a running joke from my former days as a national swimmer, and has stuck with me ever since.

But if anything, being a fish of out water has showed me that the presence and uncertainty of the unknown and stretching myself is a rewarding and thrilling prospect.

Born and raised in Singapore, I singlehandledly moved to Thailand when I was 15 and now study in the US. While the sense of displacement is no stranger to me, my significant international exposure has taught me how to navigate unfamiliar waters, adapt, connect and empathize with diverse people.

At present, it is at Duke where I live up to this trope most highly. Here, I have discovered too many previously unexplored interests, and have never felt more challenged intellectually and socially. Nevertheless, I love exploring and seeking out new experiences and charting my own course.

My Experience

Spring 2018

PTGEM

Full Stack Developer

This past Spring I worked at PTGEM, a Singaporean early-stage IOT startup. As a full stack developer, I built an in-house data analytics application for pest monitoring & management for Singapore’s Changi Airport with a Python/Django backend.

We were part of the inaugural batch of startups in Singapore Land Authority’s GeoWorks initiative, a co-innovation space launched in January 2018 to nurture local geospatial startups and connect them with corporates and industry partners. It was truly amazing experience the close interactions between Singapore's burgeoning startups and the public sector, literally on one common ground.

Summer 2017

Ministry of Trade and Industry (Singapore)

Summer Analyst

I interned in the Emerging Markets Division, and supported my team with their preparations for the then-ongoing Sri Lanka-Singapore Free Trade Agreement (SLSFTA) negotiations. I wrote political economy analyses, most notably the geopolitical implications of Sri Lanka’s Hambantota Port Deal with China, and participated in the 6th round of negotiations. At the conclusion of my internship, I delivered recommendations for continuing engagement with Sri Lankan agencies.

I gained immense appreciation for the inner workings of Singapore's civil service, and loved how my team had cultivated such a strong family-like culture. Assisting in the said negotiations also enhanced my understanding of the importance of discarding stereotypes, strengthening ties and reconciling differences between the private and public sectors.

Summer 2016 - Present

Duke-NUS Medical School

Research Assistant

I interned at Duke-NUS the summer before I matriculated into Duke, and continue to work with them to this day. I work with the Medical Education, Research & Evaluation (MERE) Team where I co-authored and am co-authoring two medical education research studies with researchers from Duke-NUS, Duke University, Yale-NUS and National Taiwan University. One study has been replicated in a pilot project with incoming first-years at the National University of Singapore in Fall 2017.

Spring 2018

Google

CodeU Fellow

I was invited to participated in the Spring 2018 cohort of Google's invite-only CodeU program. I collaborated virtually with 3 other CodeUers and a Google Engineer to build a servlets-based Java chat web app. My team and I will be presenting the app we built at Google HQ in Mountain View this August, which I am so exicted for!
