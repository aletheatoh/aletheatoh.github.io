Chain Reaction

Source Code
Demo (Warning: Sound included)
HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript
Inspiration

I have observed that the nature of gaming addition is cyclical: many turn to online games for momentary stress relief, unknowingly fall into addiction, become increasingly withdrawn from other domains of their lives, and consequently dive deeper into their addiction.

This is my attempt at creating a simple game that provides a quick and effective outlet to relieve stress, yet limits the likelihood of addiction.

Gameplay

Place the target anywhere on the canvas, and capture as many balls as possible. At any given level, you need to capture a minimum number of balls in order to advance to the next level. The larger the chain reaction you create, the more points you score!

Demo
Design

Some of my key design features were:

  • Requires minimal effort: player only makes 1 move per round
  • Fun but not too addictive: limit number of levels to 5 to reduce the time of a given playing session
  • Vibrance: integrate colorful designs and sound effects in the game
  • Blend of luck and strategy: balls will move randomly on the screen to add an element of luck and spontaneity, but player has to strategically position target to capture as many balls as possible

SumRise

Source Code
Demo
NodeJS, Express, jQuery, PostgreSQL
Inspiration

I often come across interesting articles on social media, but find that some of them are just too long and verbose; when I'm rushing for time, I just want to zero in on the main points of an article.

Ironically, I myself have a bad habit of writing too verbosely. I realized that I spend a lot of time and effort cutting down my word length in order to fulfill word / page count requirements, a task which I find tedious and menial.

I created SumRise hoping to increase my productivity in both reading and writing. I'm a huge fan of GDrive and GDocs, and SumRise is my attempt at recreating that, with the added feature of summarizing web articles and textual content.

How it works
  • For web articles, enter the URL of your web article. The text summarizer will then parse the main textual content from the web page, and generate a summarized version. It also automatically saves the article title for you.
  • For writing entries, copy and paste your writing content, and title your writing entry if you wish to. Likewise, the text summarizer will then summarize your writing content.
  • You can create folders to organize your articles/writing entries.
  • Everything is saved in the cloud: you can also edit and delete your articles/writing entries/folders.
1. User enters URL
2. Generated Summary + Title
3. User enters writing content + title
4. Edit Article
5. Edit Writing Content
6. Create folders
7. Organize into folders
8. Track your progress!

CookieTalk

Source Code
Demo
Hackathon Details
NodeJS, Express, PostgreSQL, MapBox GL JS

Winner of the Refining Residents' Experience Challenge

3rd Place Overall
Manish Nair, Clement Lee, Lim Zhao Wei
Background

My friends and I recently took part in a hackathon called 'Park of the Future' in Singapore. The theme was on envisioning a model for reinventing business parks of the future, to improve the experiences and operations of both tenants and landlords.

We won the ‘Refining Residents’ Experience’ Challenge and came in 3rd overall! It was a ton of fun, and I learnt a lot as a rookie hacker.

Our proposal

We talked to a few stakeholders, and they told us that they want a vibrant space that attracts innovative professionals, a co-space that fosters co-creation and synergies. We gathered that what the business park of today is missing is this community, and the spontaneous burst of life.

CookieTalk is a platform for tenants to organize and attend spontaneous, pop-up events within business parks. We want to create a culture of ground-up self-organisation: with CookieTalk, any resident can create their own event — and these are meant to be spontaneous, pop-up events — not centrally organised workshops or conferences.

Design

We designed an interface similar to PokemonGo. Like PokemonGo, CookieTalk provides a digital overlay over a physical space.

1. Create an event
2. Event has been successfully created
3. Clicking 'fly to event' navigates the user to the event location on the map.
Click on the map marker to see the details of the event.
4. View a list of all events, including yours
5. Indicate your interest in an event
6. Edit/Delete your events

Next Steps

We're looking to integrate "interest groups" to better connect users / residents to each other. In the longer term, we're looking to expand our target demographic to the general public and nearby institutions.

Additionally, we think that on the management level, the user information generated from CookieTalk activities may be useful in informing business parks on behavioural trends among their residents.

Ruby on Rails, React, PostgreSQL
Jian Hao Tan, Isa Tokunaga
Background

Many K-pop (Korean pop) music shows in South Korea have a tradition of crowning a “winning artist of the week,” determined by a chart system that focuses heavily on digital sales, retail sales, and social media popularity.

For example, here's the chart system breakdown for the M! Countdown Weekly Music Awards:

M! Countdown Chart System

Inspiration

In Southeast Asia where I come from, K-pop has taken the region by storm over the past two decades, and has amassed an enormous fanbase.

The idea of Ollida came about when my teammate Isa related a complaint by her friend. Her friend, a huge K-pop fan, wanted to track her idol groups’ standings in the days leading up to the winning announcements, but had to manually visit the different sources of information, a process she found tedious and troublesome. We discovered a huge pain point: there is currently no aggregator for these statistics.

Upon further research, we also noted an interesting phenomenon: these music award shows tend to exploit the psychology of fandom to build buzz and create hype. According to Billboard, the chart systems' heavy weightage on Digital / Physical Sales as well as Social Media Points has given rise to a movement of "breaking records" whereby fans race to boost YouTube offical music video views, and as crazy as it sounds, repeatedly purchase digital albums through platforms such as 1) Bugs! 2) genie 3) melon, or physical albums. For non-korean K-pop fans, buying albums may be difficult, one reason being the language barrier, and the other being the fact that they are not physically in Korea.

How it works

Ollida is a one-stop shop for K-pop fans, catered to the foreign demographic, and offers three main functionalities:

  • Aggregates various metrics such as Digital Single Sales, Album Sales, Social Media Points for K-pop Music Award Shows, and predicts nominees’ rankings in accordance to the chart system breakdowns
  • In-app marketplace connecting buyers & sellers of K-pop music albums
  • In-app marketplace connecting buyers & sellers of K-pop music albums

Demo
MERN: MongoDB, Express, React, NodeJS
Inspiration

At the end of every school year, students need to move out of the dorm for the summer holidays, and need a storage unit to store their belongings. Usually, my friends and I share a storage unit, start planning early on to get the best storage unit rental prices, and settle our own transportation. As a result, we end up saving 70-80% of the prices charged by storage companies.

The entire logistics planning process for the group is pretty tedious and menial, and is usually entirely settled by my friend, Joseph.

Inspired by Joseph (or Jo as we call him), JoJob is designed to ease the storage & moving logistics management process, catered to groups of people sharing storage units. Similar to Ollida, JoJob is a one-stop-shop for sourcing the best storage rental and rental truck offerings.
